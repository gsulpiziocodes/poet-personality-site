## 2) Positioning Doc: What Versiq Is / Is Not

## Core Positioning Statement
Versiq is a poetry identity and development platform. It analyzes recurring patterns across a writer’s poems to map them to a poetic personality profile, then helps that writer refine craft over time.

Versiq sits at the intersection of:
- creative identity (self-understanding)
- writing growth (practical improvement)
- ongoing practice (repeat submissions and profile evolution)

## What Versiq **IS**

### A pattern-based poetry analyzer
- Looks at recurring tendencies across multiple poems (imagery, emotional tone, structure, rhythm, thematic behavior, voice markers).
- Produces a **primary type**, adjacent traits, and profile-strength/confidence.

### A creative identity framework
- 16 original poet personality types across 4 families:
  - Visionaries
  - Romantics
  - Truth-Tellers
  - Makers
- Gives users meaningful language for how they naturally write.

### A growth tool, not just a result screen
- Designed to improve with more submissions.
- Encourages iteration: write -> analyze -> adjust -> write again.

### A long-term profile system
- User profile is living, not fixed.
- Changes in writing can shift profile clarity or center over time.

## What Versiq **IS NOT**

### Not a clinical or psychological diagnosis
- No mental health claims.
- No predictive claims about personality disorders or pathology.

### Not a deterministic label machine
- Type is a map, not a prison.
- Users are not boxed in permanently.

### Not a generic AI “nice poem” generator
- The value is structured interpretation and trajectory over time.
- Insight is tied to detectable writing patterns.

### Not a replacement for human editors, workshops, or literary mentors
- Versiq complements human feedback.
- It helps writers arrive at those environments with stronger self-awareness.

## Messaging Guardrails

### Say this
- “Pattern-based analysis over time.”
- “Creative profile, not fixed identity.”
- “One poem can reflect mood; many poems reveal pattern.”
- “Built to help you write with more intention.”

### Avoid this
- “Scientifically proves who you are.”
- “Definitive psychological truth.”
- “Guaranteed improvement” claims.
- “AI knows you better than you know yourself.”
